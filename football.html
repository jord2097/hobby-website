The History of Football in Sheffield

After many years of informal football kick-abouts being hosted in Sheffield, members of the pre-existing cricket clubs decided to form official football clubs. The first of which was Sheffield F.C., founded in 1857 by Nathaniel Cheswick and William Prest. In addition, they devised the "Sheffield Rules" a football ruleset which quickly caught on in Sheffield - with at least 15 clubs existing by 1860. This form of football was one of the most popular throughout the North and Midlands, competing with the FA which had its own rules and was based in the South. The Sheffield Association fought against the FA and other bodies plans to implement professionalism in the sport, but this proved to be a inevitable losing battle. The two famous Sheffield clubs, Sheffield United and Sheffield Wednesday, joined the recently formed "the Football League" in 1892.
